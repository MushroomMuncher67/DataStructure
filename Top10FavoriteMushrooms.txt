White Button: The most common type, also known as table or champignon mushrooms, are typically small and white. 
Cremini: A slightly more mature version of the white button, with a light brown color, also called baby bella. 
Portobello: A mature cremini mushroom with a fully opened, dark brown cap. 
Shiitake: A popular mushroom in Asian cuisine, with a rich, smoky flavor and distinct aroma. 
Oyster: Named for their oyster-shell shape, these mushrooms come in various colors and have a delicate flavor. 
Maitake: Also called hen-of-the-woods, these are feathery and have a strong, earthy taste. 
Enoki: Long, thin, and white, with tiny caps, often used in soups or as a garnish. 
Morel: A prized wild mushroom with a honeycomb-like appearance. 
Chanterelle: A funnel-shaped mushroom that is bright yellow or orange and has a fruity aroma. 
Porcini: Also known as cep or penny bun, these are a wild mushroom with a nutty flavor.
